Stand-up One Summary (Week 8)

User Research

We conducted user testing in the form of interviews and probes with our target users to get a better idea of the domain and their views and opinions on fake news. For this round of user testing, we were not concerned with specific designs or prototypes; as a team, we decided we needed to get a better idea of fake news itself first. Overall, based on the responses of users, we found that:

  • Fake news is a negative thing, and it can cause dangerous situations and leverage power
  • Fake news spreads faster because negative news sells
  • The only acceptable form of fake news, if any, is satire

Experience Requirements

Based on our findings from user research, our chosen social and mobile concept should:

  • Identify if a statement is true or false
  • Break a down statement into true and false pieces
  • Provide evidence of another source supporting the fact
  • Potentially ban the sharing of false statements
  • Allow the user to share true statements quicker (links and multi platform)
  • Not hinder user reading

Design Ideas

  1. Eye tracking application that fact checks news when the user physically looks at it
  2. Collaborative training application where users are given a news story and must conducted individual research to confirm its authenticity

Prototypes

We created paper prototypes based on the ideas above.

Next Steps

Before the next stand-up milestone, we aim to:

  1. Complete user testing such as interviews and probes with prototypes
  2. Begin to create higher-fidelity prototypes
  3. Conduct more research into the solution space
